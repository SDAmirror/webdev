what Gibb's free energy is?

Gibbs Free Energy is the energy available in a system to do work (reversibly, if you want to be nitpicky). That means energy that isn’t dissipated through heat or expansion of the system. This system, by the way, can be anything you want; your body, a backpack, or a specific chemical reaction. Every system has enthalpy or heat content, represented by H, which you can think of as the energy already in the system. Every system also has statistical entropy, represented by S (I’m not going to explain entropy, because there are plenty of great explanations on Quora already). Simply put, the equation G = H - TS means that the amount of energy available to do work is equal to the heat content minus the probability of energy being dissipated through entropy-related “expansion” (I use the term expansion here very liberally; the system doesn’t have to literally be expanding).
